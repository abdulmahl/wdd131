🌲 TrailWise – Community Hiking Hub

Site name rationale: "TrailWise" reflects intelligence, guidance, and a deep connection to trails & nature. The name is memorable and suggests trustworthy outdoor expertise. It positions the platform as the go-to resource for hikers, backpackers, and nature lovers seeking curated trail data and gear wisdom.

🔍 Optional domain availability: trailwise.org | trailwise.guide

📌 Site Purpose

TrailWise serves as an interactive hub for hikers and backpackers across the Pacific Northwest and beyond. The website provides detailed trail guides, real-time community Q&A, essential gear checklists, and a membership portal to access exclusive route maps. It bridges the gap between beginners and experienced adventurers, promoting safety, environmental awareness, and outdoor community connections.

🧭 User Scenarios

🗺️ “Which trails near the Columbia River Gorge offer moderate difficulty and allow dogs on-leash?”
→ A visitor planning a weekend trip with their pet wants filtered, reliable dog-friendly trail info with elevation and distance details.

🎒 “What are the top 5 lightweight backpacking food essentials for a 3-day solo trip?”
→ A solo backpacker preparing for an overnight needs quick access to expert gear lists, nutrition tips, and community-voted packing hacks.

➕ bonus scenario: “How do I join the TrailWise ambassador program to lead group hikes?” — representing community engagement & membership goals.

🔤 Typography

Montserrat

Headings: H1, H2, H3, logo, navigation titles

Clean, geometric sans‑serif for strong hierarchy.

Lato

Body text, paragraphs, scenarios, wireframe labels, and all supporting content. Highly readable on both mobile and desktop.

Italic version used for subtle notes.

Usage plan: Lato for all body copy, meta info, and buttons. Montserrat for all headings and major structural titles. No more than two font families — ensures clean performance and consistent brand identity.
